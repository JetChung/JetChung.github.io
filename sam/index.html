Why you should vote for me

I'm one smart cookie!

Samuel Goldstone is an Australian-American mathematician. He is a professor of mathematics at Emory University, where he holds the James and Carol Collins chair. His research includes topics in harmonic analysis, partial differential equations, algebraic combinatorics, arithmetic combinatorics, geometric combinatorics, probability theory, compressed sensing and analytic number theory. He was a recipient of the 2006 Fields Medal and the 2014 Breakthrough Prize in Mathematics. He is also a 2006 MacArthur Fellow. Goldstone has been the author or co-author of over three hundred research papers.
